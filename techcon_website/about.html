History

TechCon began as a small meetup in 2010 where engineers, designers, and entrepreneurs came together to share ideas about the future of technology. Over the years it has grown into a global conference that highlights breakthrough research, product launches, and community-driven initiatives. Each year TechCon builds on past successes while remaining committed to accessibility and hands-on learning.

From its modest origins, TechCon introduced focused tracks — developer tools, AI, design systems, and startup growth — allowing attendees to dive deep while cross-pollinating ideas across disciplines.

Mission

Our mission is to accelerate technology that improves lives by connecting practitioners, researchers, and builders across disciplines. TechCon is driven by three core principles:

  • Learn: Provide practical, hands-on sessions that equip attendees to ship better products.
  • Share: Amplify diverse voices in tech through curated talks and mentorship programs.
  • Build: Foster collaboration that turns ideas into real-world impact.

Notable Past Speakers

Over the years, TechCon has hosted leaders whose work shaped modern engineering and product design. Below are a few highlighted speakers and their contributions.

Dr. Ada Nguyen — Machine Learning Researcher

Dr. Nguyen's keynote on responsible machine learning helped clarify best practices for model interpretability and fairness. Her open-source toolset is used widely in industry to audit model behavior.

Samuel Ortiz — Developer Tools Lead

Samuel led a breakout on modern developer tooling that influenced the way many teams build fast feedback loops into their CI pipelines. His practical workshops remain attendee favorites.

Dr. Priya Shah — Product Design & Accessibility Advocate

Dr. Shah's session on inclusive design challenged product teams to consider accessibility early in the design process. She continues to consult on accessibility standards across multiple platforms.
